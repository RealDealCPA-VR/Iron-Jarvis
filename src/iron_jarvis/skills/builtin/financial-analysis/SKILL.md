# Financial Statement Analysis

Use this skill when evaluating a company's financial health from its statements.
The aim is a defensible read on profitability, liquidity, solvency, and trend,
grounded in the numbers rather than narrative.

## Methodology

1. **Read the three statements together.** The income statement shows
   performance over a period, the balance sheet shows position at a point in
   time, and the cash flow statement reconciles earnings to actual cash. Never
   judge profit without checking whether it converts to cash.
2. **Normalize.** Strip out one-time items, restatements, and accounting changes
   so period-over-period comparisons are like-for-like.
3. **Compute key ratios.**
   - *Profitability:* gross margin, operating margin, net margin, return on
     equity and return on assets.
   - *Liquidity:* current ratio and quick ratio.
   - *Solvency / leverage:* debt-to-equity and interest coverage.
   - *Efficiency:* inventory turnover, receivables days, asset turnover.
4. **Analyze trend and context.** Compare at least three periods to reveal
   direction, and benchmark against industry peers — a ratio is only meaningful
   relative to history and comparables.
5. **Check cash quality.** Compare operating cash flow to net income and watch
   free cash flow; persistent gaps signal aggressive accounting or distress.

## Output

State the headline assessment, support it with the most diagnostic ratios and
their trend, call out red flags (declining margins, rising leverage, weak cash
conversion), and note the limitations of the available data.
